Lab 3 Writeup - Trina Goetz

My design decisions for this lab are as follows:
    For NestedLoopJoin, I followed the structure of CrossJoin and kept a current left tuple while iterating
        through all right tuples. After going through the entire right side, I rewound it and advanced the left 
        tuple. This kept it simple and matched the iterator model well enough I think.
    For HashEqJoin, I built a hash table on the left child during open(), and used the evaluated join key as
        the key and stored a vector of tuples as the value. Then, I iterated through the right child and asked
        the hash table for matches, and stored intermediate matches to return results. This avoided recomputing 
        joins and improveed efficiency over nested loops.
    For Aggregate, I used hash-based grouping with a HashMap where the key is some group-by fields and the 
        value is intermediate aggregation results. I also kept up with a separate counts map to support averaging, 
        since it requires keepting track of both sum and count to divide at the end. I processed all the tuples 
        during open(), and then put the final results in a vector, which made next() simply just iterate over the
        results.

I spent around 4 hours on this lab, including planning/designing and implementing the required operators. The functions 
    we had to implement were very clear, and I liked how it was easy to translate the algorithms we learned in class directly
    to them once I figured out how they fit into the operator organization stuff in the repo. Aggregate was the
    hardest part, especially handling averages correctly and avoiding double-counting during initialization, which I had
    to debgug. Debugging edge cases like that took the most time, but the test cases were helpful to validate what I had
    caught and what situations I still needed to account for.
